About

Rebirth of the Griot

Rebirth of the Griot started in July 2018 with a showcase, one of it's many elements. Its goal is to bring about a renaissance of African Storytelling and spoken word. The brainchild of Sola Story who teamed up with James Bee (aka Buzzin Bee of BB3dMedia), Lady Esi and Theo Jarvis to make it a monthly event. We want to make African arts practices like drumming, spoken word and storytelling accessible to our community. We also want to use Rebirth if the Griot as a vehicle to positively impact on our community and to bring about the re-awakening of genius.

Rebirth of the Griot Storytelling and Spoken Word Showcase was founded in uly 2018. Inspired by a vision that entails: a renaissance of African oral traditions,creative learning, mentoring and cultural community engagement. We use our monthly showcase to bring our community together to support our aim and practice of offering new ‘griots’ the opportunity to perform and learn their craft alongside some of the most renowned and respected African Heritage wordsmiths in the country.

The Rebirth of Griot strap line, “the ancestors will always find a mouthpiece…” is a reference to the way in which the ancient legacy of African Storytelling inspires us to become London’s premier African Heritage storytelling performance event. Our next phase is to bring our expertise to train and promote young African Heritage storytellers. and performers. The focus will be on self-knowledge and self-realisation through self-expression.

Our next stage of continuous edutainment development includes our transformation into a Community Interest Company (CIC). To contact the Rebirth of the Griot Team:

What Is A Griot?

The griot, Djeli, Ojise or storyteller is an ancestral mouth piece, a custodian and transmitter of ancient wisdom, a ‘travelling newspaper’ a genius who uses arts and eloquence to bring people’s potential into actualisation. Wikipedia says:

“A griot, jali, or jeli is a West African historian, storyteller, praise singer, poet, or musician. The griot is a repository of oral tradition and is often seen as a leader due to his or her position as an advisor to royal personages.”

The Griot as musician, singer, storyteller has been a carrier and disseminator of wisdom traditions since at least the days of Kemet or ancient Egypt, Kush and Meroe. The nature of this role is still seen all over Africa. The importance and significance of the griot,djele, ojise, vusamazulu etc role is still recognised and practiced throughout much of Africa, especially West Africa.

However, in the diaspora among the modern day descendants of these ancient and medieval Afrikan powers this has been neglected. The art, skill and ability are not lost. Rappers, poets, musicians all have the abilities of the griot. However they often lack the purpose and therefore the spirit. Increasingly, modern day storytellers are remembering and adapting their purpose.
